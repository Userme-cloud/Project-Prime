About Project PRIME

Just as prime numbers are the irreducible building blocks of all numbers, Digital Self-Efficacy, TPACK, and Assessment Literacy are the irreducible building blocks of mathematics teacher competence.

Project PRIME is a Division-wide competence-building program designed to enhance the quality of mathematics teaching and assessment practices among secondary mathematics teachers in the Division of Aklan. Grounded in empirical research involving 213 teachers, the program addresses critical gaps in digital self-efficacy, technological pedagogical content knowledge (TPACK), and assessment literacy — with TPACK identified as the strongest predictor of assessment competence (β = .752, p < .001).

Through a progressive, evidence-based design, Project PRIME equips educators with the confidence, knowledge, and skills needed to integrate technology meaningfully into mathematics instruction and assessment, aligned with DepEd Order No. 31, s. 2020, PPST Domain 5, and the MATATAG curriculum.

Theoretical Foundations

🧠

Bandura's Social Cognitive Theory

Digital self-efficacy as the motivational foundation — teachers' belief in their capability to use digital tools determines whether they will persist through challenges and sustain technology integration in assessment practices.

💻

Mishra & Koehler's TPACK Framework

The integrated knowledge structure that operationalizes confidence through effective practice — merging Content, Pedagogy, and Technology knowledge for meaningful mathematics teaching and assessment.

📋

PPST Domain 5 & DepEd Order No. 31

Assessment literacy as the professional competency through which evidence-based instructional decisions are made — requiring teachers to design, administer, interpret, and use assessments to improve student learning.

Training Modules

Evidence-based sessions targeting identified competency gaps

Phase I — Digital Self-Efficacy (Day 1)

Building the Foundation

▼

Focus: Strengthening teachers' confidence in using digital technologies across the 5 DigComp domains, with emphasis on Problem-Solving (identified as the weakest subscale).

Session 1: Digital Foundations for Math Teachers (2 hours)

Topics: Information & Data Literacy, Communication & Collaboration, Safety

Objectives:
  • Navigate and evaluate digital resources for mathematics instruction
  • Use collaborative platforms (Google Workspace, MS Teams) for professional learning communities
  • Apply digital safety protocols when handling student data and online assessments
Activity: Digital audit of current tools
Analysis: Identifying gaps & opportunities
Abstraction: DigComp framework lecture
Application: Digital resource toolkit creation

Output: Personal Digital Competence Inventory

Session 2: Digital Problem-Solving & Content Creation (2 hours)

Topics: Digital Content Creation, Problem-Solving (priority focus area)

Objectives:
  • Create interactive digital content using Canva, Google Slides, and math-specific tools
  • Troubleshoot common technical issues in digital assessment platforms
  • Adapt when technology fails during instruction or assessment
Activity: Technology failure scenarios
Analysis: Problem-solving strategies discussion
Abstraction: Resilience & adaptability in digital contexts
Application: Digital assessment material creation

Output: Math Assessment Template (digital format)

Phase II — TPACK Integration (Days 2-3)

Connecting Knowledge to Technology

▼

Focus: Developing all 7 TPACK domains with intensive sessions on Technological Knowledge (TK) — the weakest TPACK subscale.

Session 3: Technological Knowledge for Mathematics (2 hours)

Domains: TK (priority), TCK

Objectives:
  • Demonstrate proficiency in GeoGebra for dynamic geometry and function visualization
  • Use Desmos for graphing and real-time student monitoring
  • Navigate DepEd LMS for mathematics content delivery and assessment
Activity: Hands-on tool exploration
Analysis: Tool affordances for math concepts
Abstraction: TK & TCK frameworks
Application: Interactive lesson component design

Output: Technology-Enhanced Lesson Plan (Mathematics)

Session 4: Pedagogical Integration of Technology (2 hours)

Domains: PK, PCK, TPK

Objectives:
  • Apply constructivist pedagogies using technology (inquiry-based, problem-based learning)
  • Design differentiated instruction pathways using adaptive digital tools
  • Facilitate collaborative problem-solving through digital platforms
Activity: Pedagogical strategy mapping
Analysis: Technology-pedagogy alignment
Abstraction: TPK & PCK lecture
Application: Differentiated digital activity design

Output: Technology-Integrated Pedagogical Strategy Bank

Session 5: Mathematics-Specific Technology Integration (2 hours)

Domains: CK, TCK

Objectives:
  • Use technology to teach abstract mathematical concepts (e.g., limits, transformations)
  • Select appropriate digital representations for different mathematics content
  • Design technology-based tasks that reveal student mathematical thinking
Activity: Concept mapping with tech tools
Analysis: Which tech for which concept?
Abstraction: TCK in mathematics education
Application: Math concept visualization activity

Output: Mathematics Content-Technology Mapping Guide

Session 6: Full TPACK Integration in Mathematics (2 hours)

Domains: TPACK (synthesis of all 7 domains)

Objectives:
  • Design complete mathematics lessons integrating CK, PK, and TK seamlessly
  • Evaluate technology-integrated lessons using TPACK rubrics
  • Reflect on personal TPACK growth areas and create development plans
Activity: Peer lesson review
Analysis: TPACK rubric application
Abstraction: Full TPACK framework synthesis
Application: Complete lesson redesign

Output: TPACK-Aligned Mathematics Lesson Package

Phase III — Assessment Literacy (Day 4)

Measuring What Matters

▼

Focus: Strengthening all three assessment literacy domains, with intensive work on Designing Assessments (identified as the weakest domain).

Session 7: Designing Technology-Based Mathematics Assessments (3 hours)

Focus: Designing Assessments (priority), Administering Assessments

Objectives:
  • Design assessments aligned with MELC and MATATAG curriculum competencies
  • Create items that assess both procedural fluency and conceptual understanding
  • Use digital platforms (Google Forms, Kahoot, Quizizz) for formative and summative assessment
  • Apply validity and reliability principles in technology-based assessments
Activity: Analyze existing test items
Analysis: Alignment & validity review
Abstraction: Assessment design principles (DepEd Order 31)
Application: Design valid digital assessment

Output: Technology-Based Mathematics Assessment Instrument

Session 8: Using Assessment Data for Instructional Improvement (2 hours)

Focus: Utilizing Assessment Results, Administering Assessments

Objectives:
  • Interpret digital assessment analytics to identify learning gaps
  • Use item analysis data to refine assessment quality
  • Design data-driven interventions based on assessment results
  • Provide actionable feedback to students using digital tools
Activity: Analyze sample assessment data
Analysis: Identifying patterns & gaps
Abstraction: Data-driven decision making frameworks
Application: Intervention plan development

Output: Data-Driven Instructional Intervention Plan

Phase IV — 6-Month Technical Assistance (Post-Training)

Sustaining the Growth

▼

Focus: Ensuring sustained application and continuous improvement through coaching, mentoring, and collaborative professional learning.

Components:

Monthly Coaching Sessions

  • One-on-one or small group coaching with resource persons
  • Classroom observation and feedback on technology-integrated lessons
  • Troubleshooting technical and pedagogical challenges

Learning Action Cell (LAC) Integration

  • School-based LAC sessions focused on TPACK and assessment literacy
  • Peer lesson study and collaborative planning
  • Sharing of best practices and digital resources

Action Research Support (Optional)

  • Guidance in designing classroom-based action research
  • Investigating the impact of TPACK-integrated instruction on student learning
  • Documentation and presentation of findings for professional growth

Digital Repository Development

  • Contribution to a Division-wide bank of validated digital assessment tools
  • Shared lesson plans and teaching resources
  • Continuous updating based on classroom application

Output: Portfolio of Applied TPACK Competencies + Action Research Report (optional)

Monitoring & Evaluation Plan

Based on Guskey's (2000) 5 Critical Levels of Professional Development Evaluation

1

Participants' Reactions

What It Measures: Satisfaction, relevance, engagement, and perceived value of the training

Tools: Post-session evaluation forms, feedback surveys

Timeline: End of each day and at program conclusion

2

Participants' Learning

What It Measures: Gains in knowledge, skills, and competencies (DSE, TPACK, Assessment Literacy)

Tools: Pre-test and post-test using adapted Digital Self-Efficacy Scale (Ulfert-Blank & Schmidt, 2022), TPACK Survey (Schmidt et al., 2009), and Assessment Literacy Questionnaire (researcher-made)

Timeline: Pre-test before Day 1, Post-test at end of Day 4

3

Organization Support & Change

What It Measures: School and division-level support structures that enable or hinder implementation

Tools: Administrative support checklist, resource availability audit, policy alignment review

Timeline: Monthly during 6-month TA period

4

Participants' Use of New Knowledge

What It Measures: Actual classroom application of learned competencies

Tools: Classroom observation rubric (TPACK-focused), lesson plan review, LAC documentation, portfolio assessment

Timeline: Monthly observations during 6-month TA period

5

Student Learning Outcomes

What It Measures: Impact on student numeracy performance and quality of assessment practices

Tools: Student performance data analysis, assessment quality audit (using participants' designed instruments), comparative analysis of pre- and post-program student outcomes

Timeline: End of 6-month TA period and at end of school year

Success Indicators

  • At least 80% of participants achieve "High" or "Very High" post-test scores in all three constructs
  • At least 70% of participants demonstrate sustained use of TPACK-integrated lessons in classroom observations
  • 100% of participating schools establish functional LACs focused on assessment literacy
  • Development of at least 50 validated, technology-based mathematics assessment instruments for the Division data bank
  • Measurable improvement in student mathematics performance in participating teachers' classes

About the Researcher

👤

Elvisa D. Canoy

PhD Candidate, Mathematics Education

"Cracking the Code: Math Teachers' Digital Self-Efficacy, TPACK, and Assessment Literacy as Basis for a Competence-Building Program"

This program is the culminating output of a comprehensive study involving 213 secondary mathematics teachers in the Division of Aklan. The research employed an explanatory sequential mixed-methods design, revealing that TPACK serves as the strongest predictor of assessment literacy (β = .752, p < .001), while also identifying critical gaps in digital problem-solving efficacy, technological knowledge, and assessment design competencies.

Project PRIME represents a commitment to evidence-based professional development that is responsive, data-informed, and directly aligned with DepEd policies and the MATATAG curriculum reform.
