About Project PRIME

Just as prime numbers are the irreducible building blocks of all numbers, Digital Self-Efficacy, TPACK, and Assessment Literacy are the irreducible building blocks of mathematics teacher competence.

Project PRIME is a Division-wide competence-building program designed to enhance the quality of mathematics teaching and assessment practices among secondary mathematics teachers in the Division of Aklan. Grounded in empirical research involving 213 teachers, the program addresses critical gaps in digital self-efficacy, technological pedagogical content knowledge (TPACK), and assessment literacy — with TPACK identified as the strongest predictor of assessment competence (β = .752, p < .001).

Through a progressive, evidence-based design, Project PRIME equips educators with the confidence, knowledge, and skills needed to integrate technology meaningfully into mathematics instruction and assessment, aligned with DepEd Order No. 31, s. 2020, PPST Domain 5, and the MATATAG curriculum.

Theoretical Foundations

🧠

Bandura's Social Cognitive Theory

Digital self-efficacy as the motivational foundation — teachers' belief in their capability to use digital tools determines whether they will persist through challenges and sustain technology integration in assessment practices.

💻

Mishra & Koehler's TPACK Framework

The integrated knowledge structure that operationalizes confidence through effective practice — merging Content, Pedagogy, and Technology knowledge for meaningful mathematics teaching and assessment.

📋

PPST Domain 5 & DepEd Order No. 31

Assessment literacy as the professional competency through which evidence-based instructional decisions are made — requiring teachers to design, administer, interpret, and use assessments to improve student learning.
